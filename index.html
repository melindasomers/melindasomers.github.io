About Me

Hi, welcome to my site! My name is Melinda and I hail from northern New Jersey. I attended college at UNC Chapel Hill, where I earned my Bachelor's in Neuroscience. During my time as an undergraduate, I was fortunate to gain a breadth of research experiences in the classroom and by working with several PIs who I am grateful to call my mentors. I started as research assistant with Dr. Kim Ritola helping to launch her viral vector core in the UNC Neuroscience Center. Here, I built my wet lab skills and even worked with rodent models alongside one of our collaborators to see how our viruses were used in action!

Hoping to dive deeper into working with human research subjects, I connected with Gabriella Alvarez, who at the time was a graduate student in the Psychology & Neuroscience Department, but is now an Assistant Professor of Biological & Health Psychology at the University of Pittsburgh. Under her mentorship, I helped her to launch and collect data for a placebo-controlled, within-subjects, crossover pharmacological intervention and neuroimaging study. We recruited a chronically stressed population of adults and target the inflammation in their brain (with minocycline) to investigate effects on neural reward pathways. For my senior thesis, I accumulated daily survey data from participants to compare within-subjects differences of placebo vs. active minocycline on their daily perceived stress. We found that attenuating neuroinflammation with minocycline has the potential to modulate perceived stress in chronically stressed populations.

Aiming to dive further into my passion for understanding how humans behave and adjust to a highly social world, I pursued a post-baccalaureate position in Dr. Abigail Marsh's Lab on Social & Affective Neuroscience at Georgetown University. It is here that I have had the opportunity to hone my research skills, particularly data collection, analysis, and scientific writing. Moreover, I have pinpointed my drive to explain what drives human prosociality and how can we motivate more people to connect with and help others. Looking forward, I am applying to Social Psychology PhD programs to prepare for an academic research career, where I hope to one day launch my own lab at an R1 institution.

Research Interests

empathy, altruism, prosocial behavior, wellbeing, social connection, loneliness
